Company Overview

Mixue Bingcheng is China's largest made-to-order beverage company and the second largest globally, with over 36,000 stores across 11 countries as of 2023.

Founded in 1997 by Zhang Hongchao with a small investment of RMB 3,000 ($460), the company has grown into a global franchise empire offering affordable, high-quality beverages and ice cream.

The company is currently preparing for an IPO on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, with Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, and UBS Group as joint sponsors.

Key Investment Highlights

  • Explosive store growth (doubling approximately every 2-3 years)
  • Consistent revenue growth (46% YoY in 2023)
  • Strong profit margins (51.1% net profit growth in 2023)
  • Vertically integrated supply chain with massive economies of scale
  • Successful franchising model with 16,000+ franchise partners
  • Rapidly expanding international presence in Southeast Asia

Mixue's Journey

1997

Zhang Hongchao starts with a small roadside ice stall in Zhengzhou with just RMB 3,000 ($460) in capital.

1999

Zhang's brother, Zhang Hongfu, joins the business. They rename it "Mixue Bingcheng" (Sweet Snow Palace).

2006

Introduction of affordable RMB 1 ice cream, which becomes a hit, selling up to 5,000 units a day.

2008

Restructured as a partnership, bringing in professional managers and rebranding with a new logo.

2010

Received official franchising approval from China's Ministry of Commerce, leading to rapid expansion.

2011

Partnership dissolved, leaving the Zhang brothers solely in charge of the business.

2020

Reached milestone of 18,000+ stores worldwide, including expansion into Vietnam, Indonesia, and Laos.

2021

Secured first round of financing from Hillhouse Capital and Meituan's DragonBall Capital, raising over RMB 2 billion ($278.82 million) with a valuation of RMB 20 billion.

2023

Reached 36,000+ stores across 11 countries, with record revenue of RMB 15.393 billion ($2.16 billion) in the first three quarters.

2024

Filed for IPO on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange on January 2, 2024.

Unique Business Model

Franchise-First Approach

Unlike many competitors, Mixue operates primarily through franchising (99.8% of stores are franchised). This allows for rapid expansion with minimal capital investment from the parent company.

  • Over 16,000 franchise partners worldwide
  • Lower initial investment and franchise fees than industry average
  • Only 2% of revenue comes from franchise fees
  • Waives costs for logistics, store design, and promotional materials
  • 98% franchisee satisfaction rate with 92% willing to recommend to friends/family

Affordable Pricing Strategy

Mixue's core strategy is offering high-quality beverages at extremely affordable prices (most products under RMB 10/$1.40), making it accessible to mass-market consumers.

  • Targets young consumers and residents of third/fourth-tier cities
  • Average product price of RMB 6 ($0.80) per serving
  • High volume, low margin approach enables profitability despite low prices
  • Consistent pricing strategy since founding
  • Creating category appeal to price-sensitive demographics

Vertically Integrated Supply Chain

Mixue has built China's largest and most comprehensive beverage supply chain system, a cornerstone of its "high-quality and affordable" value proposition.

  • Five major production bases covering 670,000 square meters
  • 26 warehouses with 300,000+ square meters of storage
  • Procurement network spanning 6 continents and 35 countries
  • 100% of beverage ingredients, packaging, and equipment sourced from the brand
  • 60% of ingredients are self-produced (highest in the industry)
  • 12-hour delivery reach to 90% of county-level regions in China

Revenue Model

Unlike traditional F&B businesses that rely on direct sales or franchise fees, Mixue generates most revenue through supplying ingredients and equipment to franchisees.

  • Main revenue source: Sale of raw materials to franchisees
  • Massive procurement scale enables lower costs (10-20% below industry average)
  • 2022: Procured 50,000 tons of lemons, 9,000 tons of tea, 5,000 tons of coffee
  • Franchisees benefit from lower costs, while Mixue maintains profit margins
  • Called the "interest-aligned franchise model" where franchisee success directly benefits Mixue

Is Mixue "China's Starbucks"?

While Mixue and Starbucks both dominate the beverage market in China, they target fundamentally different segments and operate on different business models:

  • Market Positioning: Starbucks is positioned as a premium, "third place" experience, while Mixue focuses on affordable mass-market beverages.
  • Scale in China: Mixue has significantly more stores in China (32,000+ vs. 7,594), demonstrating its widespread adoption.
  • Business Model: Starbucks primarily operates company-owned stores, while Mixue relies almost entirely on franchising.
  • Revenue Source: Starbucks earns directly from consumer sales, while Mixue earns primarily from supplying ingredients to franchisees.
  • Product Focus: Starbucks is centered around coffee culture, while Mixue offers a broader range of beverages with tea and ice cream being major categories.

Rather than being "China's Starbucks," Mixue represents a uniquely Chinese business innovation: a franchise-based, supply-chain-optimized beverage empire that has achieved massive scale by making quality beverages accessible to the mass market.

Investment Analysis

Strengths

Explosive Growth

Mixue has demonstrated remarkable expansion, doubling its store count every 2-3 years, reaching 36,000+ stores by 2023.

Strong Financial Performance

Revenue grew 46% YoY to RMB 15.4B ($2.16B) in the first three quarters of 2023, with net profit increasing by 51.1% to RMB 2.45B ($344.6M).

Vertically Integrated Supply Chain

Mixue's comprehensive supply chain system gives it significant cost advantages and control over product quality.

International Expansion

With 4,000+ stores internationally (primarily in Southeast Asia), Mixue has proven its model can work beyond China.

Challenges & Risks

Franchise Quality Control

Managing quality across 36,000+ franchise locations poses significant challenges for brand consistency.

Changing Consumer Preferences

As Chinese consumers become more affluent, they may shift toward premium offerings, potentially affecting Mixue's mass-market appeal.

Intense Competition

The Chinese beverage market is increasingly crowded, with both local and international players competing for market share.

Regulatory Changes

Changes in food safety regulations, franchise laws, or supply chain requirements could impact operations and costs.

Investment Conclusion

Is Mixue Worth Investing In?

Based on our comprehensive analysis, Mixue Bingcheng represents a compelling investment opportunity with its unique business model, impressive growth trajectory, and significant market position in China's beverage industry.

Key Investment Takeaways:

  • Mixue has created a unique business model that has achieved unprecedented scale in China's beverage market, leveraging franchising and supply chain integration.
  • The company's financial performance is strong, with consistent revenue and profit growth, indicating a sustainable business model.
  • With 36,000+ stores (compared to Starbucks' 7,594 in China), Mixue has demonstrated massive market penetration while maintaining growth.
  • The upcoming Hong Kong IPO provides a potential entry point for investors to capitalize on the company's continued expansion.
  • International growth, particularly in Southeast Asia, indicates potential for becoming a truly global brand.

Investment Considerations:

  • Mixue's success is built on mass market appeal and affordability, which may face challenges as consumer preferences evolve.
  • Quality control across such a vast franchise network remains an ongoing challenge.
  • The franchise-first model, while enabling rapid expansion, may limit direct revenue from store operations.

Mixue Bingcheng represents a uniquely Chinese success story that has created its own category rather than simply copying Western models. It's not "China's Starbucks" – it's something entirely new: a mass-market beverage empire built on affordability, scale, and supply chain efficiency. For investors seeking exposure to China's consumer market and beverage industry, Mixue presents an attractive opportunity with strong growth prospects and a proven business model.
